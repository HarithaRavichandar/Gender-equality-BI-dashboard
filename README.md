# Business Intelligence for Gender Equality

## Overview
This project analyzes gender disparities in the workforce using Business Intelligence (BI) techniques. It leverages large-scale workforce data to examine leadership representation, career progression, attrition patterns, and industry-wise distribution, enabling data-driven insights for organizational and policy-level decision-making.

## Problem Statement
Despite global progress toward gender equality (Sustainable Development Goal 5), disparities persist in leadership roles, promotions, and workforce participation. Traditional reporting often fails to provide actionable insights due to fragmented data. This project applies BI-driven analysis to identify and quantify these gaps.

## Dataset
- [Workplace Gender Equality Dataset (WGEA, 2023)](https://data.gov.au/data/dataset/4d35cd80-2538-4705-82f3-d0d18e823d98)
- Covers 4000+ organizations across multiple industries
- Includes workforce composition, leadership roles, promotions, and attrition metrics

## Data Preprocessing
- Handled missing values using mean/mode imputation
- Performed outlier detection for extreme values
- Standardized categorical variables for consistency
- Transformed data for analysis and visualization

## Analytical Approach
- Workforce composition analysis
- Leadership representation analysis
- Career mobility (appointments, promotions, resignations)
- Industry-wise gender disparity analysis
- Comparative analysis using BI-driven case studies

## Key Insights

### Workforce Distribution
- Workforce participation is nearly balanced:
  - Women: 51.16%
  - Men: 48.83%
- Balanced participation does not translate into equal leadership representation

### Leadership Gap
- 57.75% of leadership roles are held by men
- Women occupy a significantly smaller proportion of senior positions
- Indicates structural barriers in career progression

### Career Mobility and Retention
- Women show strong participation in hiring and early career stages
- Higher resignation rate among women (53.61%)
- Lower transition into leadership roles despite comparable promotion numbers
- Suggests a retention and advancement gap rather than a hiring gap

### Promotion Dynamics
- Leadership promotion gap of approximately 0.85% favoring men
- Reflects subtle bias in advancement opportunities

### Industry-Level Disparities
- Women dominate:
  - Healthcare (>70%)
  - Education (>60%)
- Underrepresented in:
  - Technology
  - Manufacturing
  - Mining
- Indicates strong sector-specific inequality patterns

### Critical Observation
Even in sectors with balanced workforce participation, leadership disparities persist. Workforce representation alone does not ensure equitable career progression.

## Business Impact
This project demonstrates how BI can:
- Identify hidden workforce inequalities
- Support data-driven HR and policy decisions
- Improve diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) strategies
- Enable targeted interventions in retention, promotion, and hiring
